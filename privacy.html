Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 April 2026

Readable ("we", "us", "our") is operated by Dakota Swain ABN [pending] from Queensland, Australia. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal information when you use the Readable mobile application ("the App").

By using the App, you consent to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Information We Collect

Account Information

When you create an account, we collect your email address and a password (hashed using bcrypt by our authentication provider — we do not store passwords in plain text). You may optionally provide a display name, username, bio, profile photo, banner image, favourite genres, birth year, and location (country, state or region, city).

If you sign in with Apple or Google, we receive an identity token from the provider to verify your identity. We do not store these tokens after the authentication exchange is complete.

Your country is detected once from your IP address during initial setup to determine applicable legal requirements. The IP address itself is not stored.

Reading Data

We collect information about your reading activity, including books in your library, reading sessions (start time, duration, pages read), reading goals, custom shelves, and book ratings and reviews.

Journal Entries

Journal entries are stored exclusively on your device using local storage (SQLite). They are never uploaded to our servers, never shared with third parties, and are not accessible to us. Journal data remains on your device until you delete individual entries or uninstall the App.

Social Data

If you use social features, we collect posts, comments, likes, club memberships, discussions, and follow relationships you create. You control which content is public through per-book privacy settings and profile visibility options.

Uploaded Media

Images and videos you upload (profile photos, banners, post images, review photos and videos) are stored on Cloudflare R2 (see Section 3). Image size limit: 15 MB. Video size limit: 50 MB. Video duration limit: 60 seconds. All uploaded media passes through our content moderation pipeline before publication (see Section 4).

Push Notification Tokens

If you grant notification permissions, we store your device's push notification token and your device platform (iOS or Android) so we can deliver notifications. You can revoke notification permissions at any time through your device settings.

Device Information

We collect a device identifier and platform type to manage device session limits. We do not collect Apple's IDFA (Identifier for Advertisers), advertising identifiers, or precise location data.

Usage Data

We collect information about affiliate link clicks (which store, which book, your country) to track commission earnings. We collect crash reports through Expo's default error reporting. We do not use any third-party analytics SDK, behavioural tracking, or fingerprinting.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Provide and maintain the App's features
  • Track your reading progress and generate insights
  • Display personalised book recommendations
  • Enable social features (posts, clubs, leaderboards)
  • Process Readable Pro subscriptions via RevenueCat and Apple
  • Moderate uploaded content to maintain community safety (see Section 4)
  • Generate affiliate links to bookstores (we may earn a commission)
  • Send optional push notifications (reading reminders, goal updates, social activity)
  • Comply with legal obligations

3. Third-Party Services

We share data with the following third-party services. Each service has its own privacy policy governing how it handles your data.

  • Supabase — Database hosting and authentication. Our Supabase project runs on servers in Sydney, Australia. Supabase stores your account information, reading data, social data, and all structured app data.
  • Cloudflare R2 — Media file storage. All uploaded images and videos (profile photos, banners, post and review media) are stored in a Cloudflare R2 bucket. Cloudflare stores data across its global network. See Section 6 for cross-border information.
  • OpenAI — Content moderation. We send uploaded images and optional text content (such as post captions or review text) to OpenAI's moderation API for safety screening. The image URL and text are sent; we do not send your email, name, or other personal identifiers directly, though the image URL contains a path that includes your user identifier.
  • Sightengine — Secondary content moderation for images that receive an uncertain result from OpenAI, and for all uploaded videos. The media URL is sent to Sightengine for analysis. As with OpenAI, the URL path contains your user identifier.
  • RevenueCat — Subscription management. RevenueCat receives your anonymous user identifier (a UUID) to manage your Readable Pro subscription state. It does not receive your email address or name.
  • Apple — Payment processing via StoreKit. All subscription payments are handled entirely by Apple through the App Store. Readable does not receive or store your payment details (credit card, Apple Pay, or other payment instruments).
  • Google Books API — Book search and metadata. We send search queries (title, author, or ISBN) to retrieve book information. No personal data is sent to Google through this API.
  • Expo — Push notification delivery. We send your device's push token and notification content (title and body text) to Expo's Push API, which forwards notifications to Apple's Push Notification service (APNs) for delivery to your device.
  • Amazon Australia, Bookshop.org, Booktopia, Dymocks, Angus & Robertson, and QBD — When you tap an affiliate link, you are directed to the external store's website. We do not share your personal data with these stores. The click event (which store, which book, your country, and the time) is logged internally for affiliate commission tracking.

We do not sell your personal information to any third party.

4. Content Moderation

All uploaded media (profile photos, banners, post images and videos, review photos and videos) passes through an automated moderation pipeline before it is published:

  • Text content is checked client-side against a blocklist and server-side via database triggers.
  • Images are sent to OpenAI's moderation API. If the result is uncertain, the image is also sent to Sightengine for a second opinion.
  • Videos are sent directly to Sightengine for analysis.

For each moderation check, we store a log entry that includes: the type of content, the moderation system used, category scores, the action taken (approve, review, block, or block and report), and a sha256 hash of the media file for audit and duplicate detection purposes.

If uploaded content is flagged as containing child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or violent extremism, the content is immediately deleted, the upload is blocked, and the account is flagged for review. We take these violations seriously and will comply with applicable reporting obligations.

5. Data Storage and Security

Your structured data (account information, reading data, social data) is stored on Supabase servers located in Sydney, Australia. Uploaded media files are stored on Cloudflare R2.

We use row-level security (RLS) policies on our database to ensure that users can only access their own private data through the API. Passwords are hashed using bcrypt (managed by Supabase's authentication service). All data is transmitted over HTTPS with TLS encryption.

While we take reasonable measures to protect your data, no system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Our Supabase database and authentication servers are located in Sydney, Australia. However, the following services process data outside Australia:

  • Cloudflare R2 (media storage) stores data across Cloudflare's global network, which may include servers outside Australia.
  • OpenAI (content moderation) operates servers in the United States.
  • Sightengine (content moderation) operates servers in the United States and Europe.
  • RevenueCat (subscription management) operates servers in the United States.
  • Expo (push notification delivery) operates servers in the United States.

We use these services because they provide capabilities that are not available from Australian-only providers. Each service processes only the minimum data required for its function, as described in Section 3. In accordance with Australian Privacy Principle 8, we take reasonable steps to ensure that overseas recipients handle your personal information consistently with the Australian Privacy Principles.

7. Your Rights

Under the Australian Privacy Act 1988, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data — you can view your data within the App at any time.
  • Correct inaccurate data — edit your profile, reading data, and social content at any time.
  • Delete your data — use "Delete Account" in your profile settings to permanently delete all your data (see Section 8 for details).
  • Complain to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) if you believe your privacy has been breached.

If you are located in the European Union, you also have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including the right to erasure (Article 17), the right to data portability, and the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

8. Account Deletion

When you delete your account through the App (Profile > Delete Account), the following happens immediately:

  1. All uploaded media files (profile photos, banners, post images and videos, review photos and videos) are deleted from Cloudflare R2.
  2. All your data across our database tables is permanently deleted, including your profile, reading sessions, reviews, posts, comments, likes, follows, clubs, goals, notifications, and push tokens.
  3. Your authentication record is removed and you are signed out.

If any file fails to delete in step one, the entire process stops and you are shown an error message so you can try again. We do not proceed with database deletion until all media files have been successfully removed.

Journal entries stored on your device are not affected by account deletion — they remain on your device until you uninstall the App or delete them manually.

Account deletion is immediate and irreversible. There is no recovery period.

9. Privacy Controls

You have the following privacy controls within the App:

  • Book privacy — You can mark any book in your library as private. When you mark a book as private, all associated public reviews are automatically hidden from the community feed, and the book is removed from your public favourites. This is a one-way action: making the book public again does not automatically republish hidden reviews.
  • Profile privacy — You can set your profile to private, which limits what other readers can see.
  • Push notifications — You can control which types of notifications you receive through your push notification preferences, or disable notifications entirely through your device settings.

10. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we discover that a child under 13 has provided personal information, we will delete it promptly. If you believe a child under 13 has created an account, please contact us at the address below.

11. Cookies and Tracking

The App does not use cookies, advertising trackers, or fingerprinting. We do not use Apple's IDFA (Identifier for Advertisers) or any equivalent advertising identifier. We do not participate in ad networks. We do not build advertising profiles or share data with advertisers.

12. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account, all personal data is deleted immediately as described in Section 8. There is no 30-day waiting period.

Anonymised, aggregate data (such as total reading statistics across the platform with no personal identifiers) may be retained indefinitely. Content moderation logs are retained in anonymised form after account deletion (your user identifier is removed). These anonymised logs are kept for platform safety and compliance purposes.
